PREFERENCE CENTRES

A preference centre is designed to ensure an email recipient is able to easily manage preferences of subscription. It is designed as an alternative to losing a subscription completely, instead encouraging a user to reconsider the types of emails they receive so that they are more relevant.

IDENTIFYING AND RESOLVING

My role was to help identify how best to utilise a preference centre so that a client could better facilitate a recipient's requirements. I would work closely with the customer to help build a brief that included all design guidelines and copy for the page. I would try and suggest small micro interactions that could be included to try and enhance a user's experience when using the preference centre, as simple as animating in a check icon.

The initial design would be a static mockup simply designed to illustrate my vision for the page and to help the customer understand the idea. The feedback gathered from the client could then be fed into the design to help finesse the design.

DEVELOPMENT OF A CODED PAGE

Once a final sign of of the design was received I would move forward with building the page, I often used a framework such as bootstrap or skeleton to ensure better rendering and also allow the page to be supported easier in the future if required. The page would link via API to the Pure360 platform allowing the page to pre populate with a recipient's information. This allows a returning recipient to monitor and amend preferences whenever required.

CONSISTANT RENDERING

The testing process involved using Browserstack to ensure all components rendered well or where required fell back to a suitable fallback option. I would also live test in available environments and ensure the connection between Pure360 and the page was functioning correctly.
